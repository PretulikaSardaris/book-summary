EAT THAT FROG

There just isn't enough time for everything on our 'To Do' list - and there never will be.
Successful people don't try to do everything.
They learn to focus on the most important tasks and make sure they get done.

Brian Tracy :- "In life there is not enough time to do everything. There is always more to do.
You have to stop doing few things and start focusing on important things to give them time.
Learn from successful people - they do the things only differently.""

21 ideas for improving effectiveness. Key to success is being fast.

    Your frog is your biggest task. If you have to eat 2 frogs,
    start with ugliest first – means start with most difficult task.
    Be persistent. Treat it as personal challenge. Take action immediately.
    Failure to execute is the biggest problem. It must become habit to complete the important task.
    You will confident after finishing your tasks. Practicing is the key to turn it into habit.
    Your self image impacts your performance.
    You have all abilities to learn new skills.

  • Chapter 1 Set the table Before eating your frog you need to decide your goals first means what you want to achieve. Step 1 - decide what you want.
    Step 2 – write your goals down
    Step 3 – set deadline for your goal.
    Step 4 – make a list of everything you will do to achieve your goals.
    Step 5 – organize a list into plan – set prioritize and decide which one you want to do first.
    Step 6 – take action immediately.
    Step 7 – build these activities in your schedule. Keep pushing forward. Goals are fuels in the furnace of achievement. Make a list of 10 goals for 1 year – write them in present tense as if they have come real. Now proritize which one you want to start with.
  • Chapter 2 - Plan everyday in advance Make a master list of tasks. Break down them in daily, or weekly or monthly time horizon. Prioritize your tasks while you execucte them. As you progress your tasks your motivation will go up.
  • Chapter 3 - Apply the 80/20 rule to everything Find out which task is very important. You will find out that maybe only 2 tasks are very important out of 10. Many people forget to keep to efforts for those 2 important tasks. Don’t work on tasks of low value first. Always ask which tasks are in top 20% for me and work on those. You don’t want to develop habit of working on low value tasks.
  • Chapter 4 - Consider the consequences You need to consider the long term potential consequences of doing or not doing a task. You will never have time to complete all tasks but you will have enough time to do important tasks. Questions to focus: what are my highest value activities? – align those with your stake holders. What is the most valuable use of my time for my activities?
  • Chapter 5 - practice creative procrastination As time is limited, for sure you have to procrastinate on few things. Procrastinate on those things which have less value or don’t contribute. Creative procrastination = deliberately deciding not to do few things right now.
  • Chapter 6 - Use the ABCDE method continually A items are most important, B items only have minor consequences, C tasks have no consequences, D tasks can be delegated, and E tasks can be eliminated. Now get in action.
  • Chapter 7 - Focus on key result areas Identify and determine those results that you absolutely, positively have to get to do your job well, and work on them persistently.
  • Chapter 8 - Apply the law of three Find out 3 important things that contribute the most. You can delegate or outsource rest of the tasks. Focusing on those 3 important things creates more value for you and also others. Devoting well planned time to these 3 important things is important. Avoid time losses.
  • Chapter 9 - Prepare Thoroughly Before You begin You need a neat and clean desk, have one task on the table, have everything ready to start working. It is like setting your table before your start serving your meal. Finish 80 % of the task and then improve.
  • Chapter 10 -Take It One Oil Barrel at a Time Take one bite at a time, Means take one step at a time. Your job is to go as far as possible. Make a list of everything that you have been procrastinating so far, write down everything that you can use as measure and take action.
  • Chapter 11 - Upgrade your key skills Learn what you need to learn. Continually upgrade your key skills in your key tasks. Continuous learning is a requirement in any field to be successful. 1. Each day, read 30 minutes to 60 minutes about the field or topic you're working on. 2. Join a workshop or a course to upgrade yourself 3. Listen to audio while you're behind the car. Because we spend quite a lot of time in the car.
  • Chapter 12 -Identify Your Key Constraints. Identify what is holding you back from achieving your goals. Find out for the task you're doing what are the key limits factors that are restricting you from achieving those tasks. The 80/20 rule also applies for the constraints in your life. Successful people always analyze what is holding them back. They also eliminate those limiting factors. Find out that one goal that will have a biggest impact on your life. Find out the constraint that is limiting you from achieving that goal. Why I have not achieved my goal? What is holding me back?
  • Chapter 13 - Put the Pressure On Yourself. Only 2% of the people can work without supervision, these are real leaders. Very often you wait that someone will come and motivate you. Take the responsibility in your own hands and don't wait. Put pressure at yourself to keep efforts at high level. Set yourself a deadline. Beat your own deadlines.
  • Chapter 14 -Motivate Yourself into Action. To perform best, you have to coach yourself. You have to become your own motivator. It is how you interpretate the things affect how you feel. Talk to yourself positive to boost your self-esteem. Optimism is best quality that you need for private and professional life to be successful. Optimist always look for something good in every situation. Optimists see valuable things in every setback. Optimist looks for solution in every situation. Optimistic and talk continuously about their goals. Control your thoughts. You become what you think. Keep your mind positive by taking full responsibility.
  • Chapter 15 -Technology Is a Terrible Master. The key is to keep your relationship with technology under control. Detach on a regular basis from a technology that overwhelms you to maintain your focus. People are assessing their phone in a day for 46 times to 86 times. This also goes in the direction of emails that you work on. You could reduce your efforts by only responding to important emails.
  • Chapter 16 -Technology is a Wonderful Servant. Use technology as your servant, not as your master. Make sure only the communications channel that you require are open. Block unnecessary websites. Take control of your time.
  • Chapter 17 - Focus Your Attention. Where your attention goes, your life follows. Don't check your emails early in the morning immediately. If you by mistake check your e-mail in the morning, then try to get out of that as early as possible so that you can focus. Check your e-mail only twice a day.
  • Chapter 18 - Slice and Dice the task. Use the salami technique to get the work done. Like a slice of salami try doing your project in small tasks. Once you finish small tasks is motivate you.
  • Chapter 19 - create large chunks of time. Set specific time aside for particular tasks. For example, regular reading or regular exercise or regular feedback. Plan your day in advance so that you can plan that time slot. Make every minute count.
  • Chapter 20 - Develop a Sense of Urgency. Develop impatience that drives you to achieve your tasks. Faster you move, more energy you have. Faster you move, more experience you get. Take quick actions and achieve your final end outcome faster.
  • Chapter 21 - Single Handle every task. Once you get going, keep going. Concentrate single mindedly on your tasks. Self-discipline is the key. Success in any area needs tons of discipline. Persistence is actually self-discipline and action. Discipline yourself to persist more.
